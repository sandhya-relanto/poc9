The performance of a Sales Representative is judged using a Multi-Dimensional Intelligence Matrix. This matrix combines qualitative AI analysis with quantitative behavioral metrics.

Here are the specific parameters and criteria used to judge every call:

1. Core Qualitative Metrics (Score: 0–100)
These are analyzed by the AI by comparing the transcript against the "Expert Coach" criteria defined in the system.

Empathy: Ability to demonstrate genuine understanding and validation of the customer's emotions. High score indicator: Validating concerns and showing emotional intelligence.
Objection Handling: Skill in navigating pushback. High score indicator: Staying calm, asking clarifying questions, and providing value-based resolutions.
Confidence: Tone, pacing, and authoritative presence. High score indicator: Avoiding filler words (um, ah) and maintaining a professional pace.
Listening: Ability to absorb information without interrupting. High score indicator: Referencing customer comments later in the call.
Executive Communication: Ability to speak concisely and frame value in terms of ROI/business outcomes rather than just features.
Questioning Ability: Skill in using open-ended questions (Why/How) to uncover deep pain points.
2. Behavioral Quantitative Metrics
These are "Hard Data" points calculated by the Metrics Engine.

Talk Ratio: The percentage of the call spent talking by the rep vs. the customer. (Ideally 40/60).
Question Rate: The frequency and density of questions asked by the rep.
Interactivity: How often the conversation "switches" between speakers (higher is better for engagement).
Monologue Flag: A warning triggered if the rep speaks for more than 180 words without allowing the customer to speak.
3. Strategic Intelligence Insights
The AI looks for specific "Business Milestones" in the transcript.

Tactical Successes: Specific moments where the rep used a proven sales technique effectively.
Operational Gaps: Missed opportunities or errors in the sales process (e.g., forgetting to ask about budget).
Objection Analysis: A row-by-row breakdown of every objection raised by the AI and whether the rep's response was Effective or Ineffective.
Emotional Tracking: A timeline of the customer’s emotional state (e.g., starting "Skeptical," moving to "Curious," ending "Engaged").
Highlights (Strong vs. Weak): Exact quotes from the rep that are flagged as "Golden Moments" or "Coaching Opportunities."
4. Outcome Analysis
The final "Verdict" on the session.

Overall Score: The weighted average of all core metrics.
Outcome Summary: A narrative explanation of why the call succeeded or failed.
Next Practice Recommendation: A tailored suggestion for the next scenario the rep should practice to fix their specific weaknesses.
